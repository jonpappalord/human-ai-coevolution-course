Course description

The rise of socio-technical systems, where humans interact with AI assistants and recommenders, poses risks of unintended consequences. Navigation apps like Google Maps may cause congestion by directing too many drivers to the same route; profiling and targeted ads can reinforce biases and inequality; and generative AI chatbots risk declining quality as synthetic data increasingly drives retraining. These issues stem from Machine Learning models trained on human behaviour, creating feedback loops that shape future choices and data. This course equips students to analyse and model these loops, designing experiments to assess their societal impact and foster responsible AI development.

Expected skills (learning outcomes)

  • Design experiments to assess the impact of AI algorithms in human–AI ecosystems.
  • Measure and interpret the impact of AI algorithms on individuals and society.
  • Model feedback loops between humans and AI algorithms.

Assessment

Students will submit a document describing experiments (empirical or simulations) to assess AI impact in ecosystems like social media, retail, and urban mapping. The document must outline:

  1. Which aspects of impact are measured;
  2. How the experiment(s) are designed;
  3. Key findings from existing literature relevant to the chosen ecosystem and impact dimension.

The document will be presented by the student during an oral presentation.

Course syllabus (sessions)

  1. Introduction to Human–AI Coevolution. Overview of the course topic, delivered as a lecture with interactive discussions.
  2. The Human–AI Feedback Loop. Exploration and formalisation of feedback loops in ML models, with examples from social media, online retail, urban mapping, and chatbots.
  3. Types of Experiments. Overview of experimental methods to evaluate feedback-loop impacts, illustrated with examples from diverse human–AI ecosystems.

  5. Unintended Consequences in Social Media. Case studies on platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube.
  6. Unintended Consequences in Online Retail. Real-world examples on platforms like Amazon and Spotify.
  7. Unintended Consequences in Urban Mapping. Effects on platforms such as Google Maps and Airbnb.
  8. Unintended Consequences in Chatbots. Challenges posed by AI-generated chatbots like LLaMA and ChatGPT.
  9. Open Challenges in Human–AI Coevolution. Unresolved technical, legal, and political issues in measuring human–AI coevolution.
  10. Practice Session. Hands-on activity: designing experiments to assess impacts of AI recommenders (exam preparation).
  11. Exam / Presentations (I). Student presentations and evaluation.
  12. Exam / Presentations (II). Student presentations and evaluation.
